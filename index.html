Olympic Weightlifting, What is it?

Technically speaking, Olympic Weightlifting, otherwise known as ‘Weightlifting’ or ‘Olympic-style Weightlifting’ is one of the oldest sports which incorporates the use of two independent lifts which require the athlete to lift a loaded barbell from the floor to an overhead position in an explosive manner. The two lifts, the ‘Clean & Jerk’ and the ‘Snatch’ are explosive movements as they require a combination of maximal strength and maximal speed. On the other hand, these exercises and their variations are an effective method for enhancing athletic performance. Sports that require high-load speed strength such as football, volleyball, basketball, and track and field, have all been suggested to benefit from the use of Olympic Weightlifting because of their biomechanical characteristics of high force and power output.
